This page is intended to be a place where I review anything I can find in a serious manner. You may find me reviewing the art available in the local mega-store or the latest catchy commercial jingle. I truly will not let anything become off limits to me.

I do take requests! Please feel free to send any material you would like to see me review to the email address listed below on my contact card! The more absurd items, and in general worse quality media will take precedence over more polished works. I take pride in my ability to paint a vivid picture from as little as possible.

About Me:

Michael Grossman (he/him/his) is a twenty-seven-year-old art enthusiast, hackathon coder, and cat-lover currently working full time and studying Computer Science full time at Queens College. He has been working with his current employer for seven years and has worked several positions, namely Warehouse Manager, Purchasing Agent, and now E-Commerce Manager. At Queens College he is currently a Junior with a 3.98 GPA and has experience with programming in C++, Java, Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. For the 2021 CUNY Start-ups Hackathon he has proudly worked with and managed a team that has produced a Java and Python based program that receives satellite images and detects/predicts relevant information for forest fires. He has also built a command line phone book in C++, and a toy version of software intended to run a vending machine.

Whether working alone or in a team Michael can meet goals and complete projects in more than acceptable timeframes. He naturally fits into the leadership role with scholarly peers as he is on average a bit older and more experienced with group management. The projects he is most excited to take on in the future are those concerning Natural Language Processing, Artificial Intelligence, and Quantum computing. Ideally, he sees himself working in a research environment for the above as he finds them extremely fascinating topics and is eager to delve further into them academically.
