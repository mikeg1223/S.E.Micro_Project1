Title: Dirty Little Virus

Artist: Iggy Pop

Date: 12/21/2020

Article Author: Michael G.

In one of the more surprising moves one came make during a year wrought by pandemic and economic disaster, Iggy Pop has released a song which can only be described as “the conjunction of a lack of effort with a lack of creativity.” In this song Iggy Pop attempts to sing to us over some slow bluesy riffs and professes his troubles with the novel coronavirus of 2019, many of which have been affecting most of the population. While these topics on their own seem to be fine material for a song, the list of problems starts to rear it’s head when Iggy opens his mouth and begins to sing. In what some may describe as a drunken mumble Iggy paints us a timeless picture with unforgettable lines such as “She’s only nine-teen, but she can kill you. She ain’t my type, but it is what it is. Guy get’s a fever, and thinks he see her. Oh what a grind, I’m losing my mind.” With one glance at those lyrics, one can see that Iggy himself may be suffering from a fever right now.

As the song progresses it seems that Iggy himself was trying desperately to incorporate a spacey sound to the interludes of his music that he fails to execute properly. Similar to the drunken stumble that is his voice, these spacey sections of his song invite the listener to experience blacking out without intoxication as they are filled with the off-balance noises of a failed attempt. While Iggy Pop may very well have been quite the performer in his youth, it is becoming obvious that his poorly aged taste is now showing, unfortunately.
