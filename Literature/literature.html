Title: Synopsis of American Gods: A Novel Paperback – March 28, 2017 on Amazon.com

Writer: Unknown

Date: Unknown

Article Author: Michael G.

Of the thousands of different synopses of books out there, this one stands out to me as the only one I’ve ever reviewed. In many ways the format of this synopsis is fairly run of the mill. It tantalizes the reader with tidbits of information. Just enough so they will feel compelled to open the book, but not so much that they will ascertain the story. One of the more interesting choices this writer made was to omit the mention of gods in the synopsis, albeit in the title. This leaves the reader in a state of wonder and awe. Some questions may come to mind such as: Where do gods fit into this? And why are they American? aren’t gods supposed to be above human concepts such as nationality?

If the reader surpasses these questions and finds themselves at the end of the main synopsis they are in for the real treat. Tucked into the bottom of the synopsis the write made the bold stylistic choice of adding a review by the Washington Post. While this section has little to give in terms of the story, it does lend itself to the art of sales and suggests the writers mastery over content and its conjunction with marketing in a move that some may consider a tad tasteless. Overall, this synopsis serves its purpose as intended for a book which I would recommend to anyone reading this.
